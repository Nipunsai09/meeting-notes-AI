Meeting: Q3 Product Roadmap Review
Date: April 28, 2025

Meeting Purpose:

To lock in Q3 product priorities and align on the dashboard redesign before engineering sprint planning commences next week.

Key Takeaways:

  • Two Q2 carryover tickets (export feature and notification preferences) are prioritized as P0 for Q3.
  • The dashboard redesign will adopt a widget-based layout, with an initial launch of five core widgets targeted for mid-Q3.
  • The mid-Q3 dashboard redesign release will align with the annual conference in early August for maximum marketing impact.
  • A proposal will be developed to address the 34% drop-off in the onboarding flow by redesigning the "invite team" step.
  • API v1 will be deprecated by year-end, with customer notifications planned for July to allow for a 90-day transition period.

Topics (Detailed Breakdown):

Q2 Carryover Tickets

  • Discussion Point: Two tickets, the export feature and notification preferences page, were not completed in Q2 and are being carried over.
  • Problem: Unfinished work from the previous quarter impacting new development.
  • Solution: These are flagged as P0 priorities for Q3, meaning they will be addressed immediately.
  • Design Status: Priya confirmed export designs are done; notification preferences mobile states require two more days, with completion targeted by Thursday for Marcus's team.

Dashboard Redesign

  • Problem: Enterprise customers (40 surveyed) find the current dashboard layout difficult because it surfaces all metrics by default instead of allowing users to configure their view.
  • Solution: The new design will implement a widget-based layout, enabling users to pin, remove, and reorder panels.
  • Initial Scope: Six widget types were proposed (Revenue summary, active users, churn rate, recent activity feed, goal tracker, custom metrics panel).
  • Phasing Decision: The "custom metrics panel" will be deferred to a follow-up release due to its estimated three weeks of complex backend engineering work.
  • Target Launch: The first five widgets are estimated to take ten days of engineering work and are targeted for a mid-Q3 release, which aligns perfectly with the annual conference in early August for showcasing.
  • Marketing Needs: Tom requires two weeks lead time for launch assets (email, in-app announcement, landing page) and needs final screenshots by July 7th for a target release week of July 21st.

Onboarding Flow Optimization

  • Problem: A significant 34% drop-off occurs at the "invite your team" step in the onboarding flow. This is likely because users are asked for a commitment before they've experienced product value.
  • Proposed Solution: Move the "invite team" step to occur after the first dashboard is set up, framing it as a natural progression rather than a mandatory gate.
  • Next Steps: Priya will spec this out as a separate initiative for potential Q4 implementation and provide a proposal to share with the executive team.

API Versioning and Deprecation

  • Problem: Maintaining both API v1 and v2 in parallel for eight months has led to significant overhead for the engineering team.
  • Solution: Sunset API v1 by the end of the year to reduce maintenance burden.
  • Timeline: A 90-day customer notification period is required. Sending the deprecation notice in July would allow for a sunset in October.
  • Action: Tom will draft the customer-facing deprecation communication, requiring technical details from Marcus.
